Welcome to BeanArts. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This privacy policy will inform you about how we look after your personal data when you visit our website (regardless of where you visit it from) and tell you about your privacy rights and how the law protects you.

By using our corporate gifting and printing services, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

1 Information We Collect

We collect several types of information for various purposes to provide and improve our services to you, especially regarding corporate gifting, printing, and packaging solutions.

  • Identity Data: Names, usernames, and contact details (email addresses, phone numbers) used for placing orders and communication.
  • Transaction Data: Details about payments to and from you and other details of products and services you have purchased from us (e.g., recipient names/addresses for bulk gifts).
  • Technical & Usage Data: IP addresses, browser type, time zone setting, and information about how you navigate our website.

2 How We Use Your Personal Data

We use your data for the following primary purposes:

  • To fulfill and manage your orders for corporate gifts, printing, and packaging.
  • To manage our relationship with you, including notifying you about changes to our terms or policy.
  • To administer and protect our business and this website (including troubleshooting, data analysis, testing, and hosting).
  • To deliver relevant website content and measure the effectiveness of our services.

3 Sharing Your Personal Data

We may share your personal data with trusted parties for specific purposes:

  • Third-Party Service Providers: Logistics partners (for delivery), payment processors, and IT support services.
  • Legal Compliance: When required by law to protect the rights, property, or safety of BeanArts or others.

4 Data Security

We have put in place appropriate security measures to prevent your personal data from being accidentally lost, used, or accessed in an unauthorized way. Access to your personal data is limited to employees and partners who have a strict business need to know.

5 Your Legal Rights

Under certain circumstances, you have rights under data protection laws in relation to your personal data, including the right to request access, correction, erasure, or restriction of processing.
